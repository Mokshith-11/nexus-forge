# The Cold DM Method (how to actually do it)

Messages alone don't close — the *way* you send them does. Follow this.

## 0. Set up so you don't get banned (do this first)
Cold-messaging unknown numbers from a normal WhatsApp can get your number **flagged/banned**. Protect yourself:
- Use **WhatsApp Business** (free app) — set a profile: name "Nexus Forge", logo, website nexusforge.in, "AI Automation Agency".
- **Warm the number up**: don't blast 61 at once. Day 1: ~15–20. Build up over days.
- **Save the contact first** before messaging when you can (reduces spam signals).
- **Vary the message slightly** each time (the DMs file already differs per business) — identical copy-paste to 60 numbers looks robotic to WhatsApp.
- Never paste a link in the *first line*. (Our DMs put nexusforge.in at the end on purpose.)
- If someone says "stop"/not interested → stop. Don't re-message.

## 1. The structure of a reply-getting DM (P-P-O-A)
Every message follows 4 beats, in 3–4 short lines:
1. **Personalize** — name the business + something specific ("11k reviews", "saw your listings"). Proves it's not mass spam.
2. **Pain** — one sharp problem they feel ("who replies to WhatsApp leads after hours?").
3. **Offer** — the outcome + zero risk ("AI assistant that replies in seconds & books — free 7-day pilot").
4. **Ask** — one simple question ("want one for [business]?").

Short. No paragraphs. No jargon. End with a question, not a link.

## 2. Daily routine (the engine)
Each day, ~60–90 min:
- **Send 15–25 new DMs** (from cold-dms.md, top-down by priority).
- **Follow up** everyone you messaged 24h and 72h ago.
- **Reply within minutes** to anyone who responds (speed = the deal).
- **Update the tracker** status for every row you touch.

Numbers that work: ~100 DMs → ~15–25 replies → ~5–8 calls → 1–2 pilots → 1 paid client. Volume is the lever.

## 3. The follow-up sequence (where most deals come from)
80% of replies come AFTER the first message. Cadence:
- **Day 0:** first DM.
- **Day 1 (no reply):** "Just floating this back up 🙂 free pilot's open this week, ~1 day to set up, costs nothing. Quick call?"
- **Day 3 (no reply):** "Last nudge 🙂 if WhatsApp lead replies are ever a headache, the free pilot's here. Just say the word."
- **Day 7:** leave it. Move on. (Re-touch in a month.)

Three touches, then stop. Polite, never pushy.

## 4. When they reply — what to say
- **"How much?"** → "There's a free 7-day pilot first — you only pay (₹15–25k/mo) if it works. Can I set it up? Just need your WhatsApp number + a 10-min call. Free today/tomorrow?"
- **"Send details"** → send 2–3 lines + the proposal (proposal-template.md). Then ask for the call.
- **"Not interested"** → "No worries! Mind if I check back in a month?" (keeps the door open).
- **Goes quiet after interest** → follow up next day: "Still keen to get your free pilot live this week — shall I start?"

Goal of every reply = **book the 10-min call.** Don't try to close over text; close on the call.

## 5. On the call (close in 4 beats — from outreach-scripts.md)
1. Diagnose their lead flow. 2. Quantify the cost of missed leads (₹). 3. Show the WhatsApp demo on nexusforge.in. 4. "I can have your free pilot live in 48h — what's the best number?" Anchor the paid price for after the week.

## 6. Channel notes
- **Clinics / real estate / fitness** → WhatsApp (numbers in tracker). Best reply rates in India.
- **Coaches** → also try **Instagram DM** (comment/like a post first, then DM — warmer).
- **Landline-only leads** → call, don't text (script in cold-dms.md).
- **No-website leads** → bonus pitch: "I can build you a site too."

## 7. Track everything
Open lead-tracker.csv. For every lead: set Status (New → Contacted → Follow-up 1/2 → Call Booked → Pilot Running → Closed). If it's not in the tracker, it didn't happen. The follow-ups are where the money is — the tracker makes sure none slip.
