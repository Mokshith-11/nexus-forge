# The First-Client Master Plan (7 Days)

The veteran truth: first clients don't come from better tools, more leads, or a prettier site.
They come from **proximity + proof + a small ask**, repeated daily. You already have the proof
(nexusforge.in/demo). This plan is the proximity and the ask.

---

## The 3 rules (break these and the plan fails)

**Rule 1 — Shrink the ask.**
Nobody gives a stranger ₹20k/month. Everybody risks ₹5–8k once.
First-client offer: **"₹7,500 flat. AI assistant live on your WhatsApp in 5 days. If you love it, ₹15k/month to keep it running. If not, we part as friends."**
On Upwork: **$75–150 fixed** for the first job. The goal of client #1 is not profit — it's a testimonial, a case study, and momentum. You raise prices from client #3.

**Rule 2 — Three channels only. Daily. No new tools.**
Chosen: **(A) Warm network + local, (B) Upwork, (C) LinkedIn/replied leads.**
Ads, agents, scrapers, email setups — all banned for 7 days. Building is procrastination now.

**Rule 3 — The fortune is in the follow-up.**
Every conversation gets 3 touches: Day 0, Day 1–2, Day 4–5. Track every name.
Most first clients say yes on touch 2 or 3, never touch 1.

---

## The daily engine (2–3 hrs/day, same blocks every day)

| Block | Time | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 30 min | **Warm/local:** 5 personal messages or 2 calls/walk-ins with the phone demo |
| 2 | 45 min | **Upwork:** bid on 8–10 fresh jobs (sort newest; bid within 15 min of posting) |
| 3 | 30 min | **Follow-ups:** everyone from 1–2 and 4–5 days ago |
| 4 | 15 min | **Replies:** answer every response within minutes; push each to a 10-min call |
| 5 | 15 min | **Log it:** update the tracker. If it's not written, it didn't happen |

---

## Day-by-day

**Day 1 — Warm blitz.** List 20 people you know. Message 10 (scripts in cold-dms/marketplace kit).
Finish Upwork profile ($25/hr, ML overview). Bid on 5 jobs tonight.

**Day 2 — Local + Upwork.** Message remaining 10 warm. Visit/call 3 local businesses with the
phone demo. 10 Upwork bids. Follow up Day-1 non-repliers.

**Day 3 — Partners.** DM 5 web designers / marketing freelancers: 20% referral for AI clients they bring.
10 Upwork bids. Follow-ups.

**Day 4 — LinkedIn Post 2** (AudioGuard/ML credibility — already written). DM everyone who reacted
to Post 1. 10 Upwork bids. Follow-ups (touch 2 for Day 1–2 people).

**Day 5 — Groups + real-time.** Answer in 3 FB/WhatsApp owner groups; search X/Reddit for
"need a chatbot/website" and reply with the demo. 10 bids. Follow-ups.

**Day 6 — Audit videos.** Record 2 two-minute audit videos (script in audit-outreach.md) for the two
warmest prospects so far. Send. 10 bids. Follow-ups (touch 3 closes happen today).

**Day 7 — Harvest.** No new outreach. Only follow-ups and calls. Push every warm thread to a
yes/no with the shrunken offer. Review numbers; keep the two channels that produced replies.

## When someone replies (the close path)

1. **Reply within minutes.** Speed is the #1 conversion factor.
2. **Send the demo, ask one question:** "Try this 20-sec demo — nexusforge.in/demo. Want this on your WhatsApp?"
3. **Get to a 10-min call.** Never negotiate in text.
4. **On the call:** ask about their lead flow → what a missed customer costs → show demo → offer:
   "₹7,500, live in 5 days, then ₹15k/month only if you love it. Shall I start Monday?"
5. **Objections:**
   - "Too costly" → "What does ONE missed customer cost you? This pays for itself with the first booking."
   - "Need to think" → "Of course. I'm taking two setups this month — can I check back Friday?"
   - "Will it work for us?" → "That's what the first week proves. If it doesn't, you've lost nothing."
6. **After every call, same day:** send the proposal (proposal-template.md) + "shall I start?"

## The math (why this works)

7 days × (10 warm/local + 10 bids + follow-ups) ≈ **120–150 touches**
→ 15–25 conversations → 4–6 calls → **1–2 closed at the small offer.**
Every "no" is a paid step toward the yes. The plan only fails if the reps stop.

## Forbidden this week
New tools/agents/scrapers · new channels · redesigning anything · quoting monthly retainers to
strangers · waiting more than 5 min to answer a reply · stopping after one "no".
